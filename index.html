ml+aging

We were able to train multiple supervised classifier models on a single-cell RNA-sequencing dataset to predict whether a blood stem cell is young or aged. Using in-house feature selection algorithms, we retroactively discovered small, critical subset of gene clusters that potentially drive aging, and can predict the age of a stem cell with 98.5% accuracy. We are currently exploring the biocomputational and therapeutic implications of our study.

Our work is done in collaboration with the Stanford CS department and Stanford Medical School, and our paper is available upon request.

3D tissue modeling

I created a workflow for analyzing 3D fluorescent images from laser-scanning confocal microscopy. This allowed the quanfication of cellular relationships within a tissue sample (such as cell-cell distance, density, # of branch points of blood vessels), and the algorithm was adopted by the rest of the lab.

The project was sponsored by Longaker Lab and the Stanford Stem Cell Institute. Our manuscript is under review.
